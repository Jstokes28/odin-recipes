Mapo Dofu

Description

A delicious Sichuanese dish from, well, Sichuan province, Mapo Doufu features rich, savory flavors and a cool, stinging heat.

The roots of the dish are largely unknown, but rumor holds that a pockmarked old woman threw together ingredients from what she had on hand to satiate a traveling soldier. The became one of the most famous tofu dishes in not only China, but worldwide.

Ingredients

  • Tofu, preferably silken
  • Ground pork - ground beef or a mix of mushrooms and walnuts can substitute
  • Pixian Doubanjiang, or 'broad bean paste'
  • Spring onions
  • Douchi or 'fermented black beans'
  • Garlic
  • Fresh ginger
  • Sichuan peppercorns (at least a teaspoon)
  • Cooking oil
  • Fresh rice!
  • Broth - chicken is traditional
  • Corn starch
  • Chinese hot oil

Instructions

  1. Blanch tofu in boiling water to reduce breakage
  2. Chop garlic, mince ginger
  3. Sizzle aromatics
  4. Add ground meat, cook until most of the way done
  5. Add Sichuan peppercorns
  6. Add pixian doubanjiang, appreciate delicious smell
  7. Add douchi
  8. Add chicken broth to make it more of a soup
  9. Add cornstarch
  10. Add Chinese hot oil
  11. Add tofu
  12. Cook for 3-5 minutes for tofu to take in those delicous flavors
  13. Add scallions
  14. Cook until scallions are just wilted
  15. Garnish with more Sichuan peppercorns and scallions
  16. Serve over hot rice!
