Borscht

Description

A beet based dish with as many variations as there are stories of its origin.

The roots of the dish are contested between Slavic countries. Traditionally served with bread. In Ukraine, this is generally garlicky and in roll form - but while I CAN make bread, I generally eat it as-is. It's also traditionally made with meat, but I'm too lazy to cook ground meat more than I have to. Mine is vegetarian, and if you make it without the sour cream, it becomes a lovely light (and vegan!) soup.

Ingredients

  • Beets
  • Potatoes, if desired
  • Stock - I use beef, usually
  • Sour cream for garnish /li>
  • Carrots
  • Cabbage
  • Pretty much whatever vegetables you have on hand
  • Lemon juice
  • Dill
  • Oil to add with the stock

Instructions

  1. You ready for this? Really, it's easy. Simplest soup.
  2. Add a load of stock to the pot
  3. Add the beets
  4. Add the potatoes - if desired. They can make your soup cloudy.
  5. Add your carrots
  6. Bubble away at it at whatever heat you like until the potatoes or carrots or beets are cooked through
  7. This is even easier if you grate the beets in question!
  8. Toss in a bunch of dill - as much as you like
  9. To up the acidity, add lemon juice
  10. Add a bunch of salt and a bunch of pepper
  11. Serve with a dollop of sour cream and sprinkling of dill
  12. Be careful to not splash it - borscht is a professional stainer.
